Risk Management on Bybit — Stop Loss & Take Profit Done Right

📖 6 min📅 April 2025🔥 Most Popular

Most intermediate traders understand what a stop loss is. But understanding the concept and using it correctly are two very different things — and the gap between them is what separates consistent traders from those who blow their accounts.

Why Most Traders Set Stop Losses Wrong

The most common mistake is placing a stop loss based on how much money you're willing to lose, rather than where the market would actually invalidate your trade idea.

Your stop loss should be placed at the point where your trade thesis is proven wrong — not at an arbitrary dollar amount.

Setting a Stop Loss on Bybit

  • Toggle TP/SL on the Bybit order form.
  • Enter your Stop Loss price below a key support level.
  • Set Take Profit at minimum 1:2 risk-to-reward.
  • Use Mark Price triggers for futures to avoid wick manipulation.

How to Set Up Bybit Like a Pro

📖 5 min📅 April 2025⚙️ Setup

The default Bybit setup is designed for the average new user. Here are 7 settings to change immediately as an intermediate trader.

1. Switch to Professional View

Enable the "Pro" trading view to unlock full order book depth, advanced chart layouts, and detailed position data.

2. Set Default Leverage

Bybit defaults to maximum leverage. Set a sensible default (5x–10x) in your futures settings to avoid accidental over-exposure.

Leverage discipline is the single biggest separator between traders who survive long term and those who don't.

3. Switch Stop Losses to Mark Price

Mark Price triggers protect against wicks that don't reflect the true market price. Switch this in your order settings.
